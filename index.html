INCT-CERBC Promotes the 23rd INFOCUS and 2nd ISHAM LATAM em Curitiba

INCT-CERBC is proud to promote the 23rd INFOCUS Congress and the 2nd ISHAM LATAM Congress, which will take place in Curitiba, Brazil, from July 22-25, 2026, at the Fiep Events Center.

Organized by Flavio Queiroz Telles, Vânia Aparecida Vicente (INCT-CERBC coordinator) and Arnaldo Lopes Colombo, INFOCUS is internationally recognized for scientific quality, addressing the main challenges in medical and veterinary mycology. The event will include Educational Workshops and the Future Leader Campaign, sponsored by ACCORD.

Scientific Training
4th International Course on Medical Mycology in Manaus

The International Course on Clinical Mycology, offered by the Westerdijk CBS Institute/Netherlands and currently by the Atlas of Clinical Fungi Foundation, held its 4th edition in Brazil from December 1-8, 2025, at ILMD–Fiocruz Amazonia, with local coordination by Drs. Ani B. J. Matsuura and Vania A. Vicente.

With support from INCT-CERBC, PPGVIDA and LDMAIS/Fiocruz, the course adopted a hybrid model: online theoretical module broadcast live to regions in Northern Brazil and countries such as Venezuela and Colombia; in-person modules with 100 slots for theoretical classes and 60 for practical sessions.

In total, 89 participants completed the training: 83% were health professionals, bioscientists and surveillance personnel, and 17% were graduate students. Taught by Prof. Sybren de Hoog and collaborators such as Jacques Meis and Flavio Queiroz-Telles Filho, the course offered molecular tools for identification and diagnosis of fungi applied to Biotechnology and public health.

INCTs Highlighted at Araucária Week of Science, Technology and Innovation

The Araucária Week of Science, Technology & Innovation, concluded in Curitiba, established itself as a milestone for Paraná's scientific development: more than 1,200 participants and about 250 scientists over three days of programming with presentations from more than fifty collaborative networks and strategic projects.

The highlight was the presentation of the INCTs (National Institutes of Science and Technology). Paraná is experiencing a historic moment, jumping from 3 to 14 INCTs — strengthening made possible with resources from the Araucária Foundation. "We perceive a very positive environment for science in the State, with the scientific community motivated," highlighted President Ramiro Wahrhaftig.

Among the highlights: the INCT NanoVida with advances in solar cells and biosensors; the INCT in Sustainable Engineering and Marine with research on pollution from heavy metals and nanoplastics; and the INCT of Biorefinery of Lignocellulosic Materials with biodegradable packaging. The 14 Paraná INCTs work in biotechnology, marine bioeconomy, sustainable agriculture, health, nanotechnology and freshwater ecology.
